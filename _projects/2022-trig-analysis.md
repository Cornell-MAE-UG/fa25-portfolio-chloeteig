## **Overview**
We founded ModuBaby to rethink how child safety products are designed, used, and discarded. Our flagship product, EverSeat, is a modular child car seat that adapts from infancy through the booster stage, eliminating the need for families to purchase multiple seats as their child grows.

We designed EverSeat to address three core problems in the current market: poor caregiver accessibility, unnecessary replacement-driven waste, and bulky designs that prioritize convenience features over long-term usability. Rather than treating each growth stage as a separate product, we built EverSeat as a single adaptive platform that grows with the child.

---

## **Modular Concept**
We built EverSeat around a single structural frame that supports multiple seating configurations over time. Instead of relying on detachable accessories that can be lost or misused, we intentionally repurposed core components across stages to reduce part count and simplify transitions.

<img src="{{ '/assets/images/seat-evolution.png' | relative_url }}" style="max-width: 650px; display:block; margin: 1.5rem auto;" />

This modular architecture allows the seat to adapt to a wide range of child sizes while maintaining consistent geometry, predictable load paths, and intuitive adjustment. By designing the system as a platform rather than a sequence of products, we reduced both user error and long-term material waste.

---

## **Engineering & Safety**
We designed EverSeat around a lightweight carbon-fiber composite shell to maximize strength-to-weight performance and long-term durability. Shared interfaces and adjustable geometry allow the structure to remain continuous and mechanically consistent across all configurations.

We evaluated structural feasibility using ANSYS under FMVSS 213–inspired crash loading conditions. Our simulations showed factors of safety greater than 2.5 across every seating configuration, confirming that modularity does not compromise safety.

We used a Design Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (DFMEA) to guide design decisions throughout the project. This process helped us identify and mitigate risks related to installation errors, accessibility limitations, and long-term wear before physical testing.

---

## **Sustainability**
We treated sustainability as a primary design constraint rather than an afterthought. By designing EverSeat to remain in service for significantly longer than traditional car seats, we directly reduced material waste and repeated manufacturing.

We selected materials that balance durability, comfort, and environmental impact, including merino wool fabrics, bio-based polyurethane foam, cork, and carbon fiber. A lifecycle assessment using Sustainable Minds showed a meaningful reduction in CO₂ impact compared to conventional multi-stage car seats, validating the environmental benefit of a long-lifespan, modular system.

## **Business Case**
In parallel with technical development, we built ModuBaby as a venture-backed startup concept around EverSeat. I led the development of the business case, including market research, competitive benchmarking, cost modeling, pricing strategy, and investment planning.

We positioned EverSeat as a premium direct-to-consumer product designed to replace multiple traditional seats over a child’s lifetime. Our financial model and break-even analysis showed a clear path to profitability after accounting for certification, tooling, and early manufacturing costs.
